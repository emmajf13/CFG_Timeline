THE HISTORY OF MEMES

meme

[miːm]noun

1. an element of a culture or system of behaviour passed from one individual to another by imitation or other non- genetic means. 2. an image, video, piece of text, etc., typically humorous in nature, that is copied and spread rapidly by Internet users, often with slight variations.

This website is dedicated to pay tribute to the best Memes of our Time

2017

Evil Kermit

Source

In 2014, the musical comedy film Muppets Most Wanted was released, in which the character is confronted by his look-a-like Constantine dressed in a black cloak. On November 6th, 2016, Twitter user @aaannnnyyyyaaaa tweeted a screenshot of Kermit the Frog standing next to a Sith version of himself with the caption “me: sees a fluffy dog / me to me: steal him” (shown below). Within 10 days, the tweet gained over 31,800 likes and 22,500 retweets.

2012

Overly Attached Girlfriend

Origin

On the 6th of June 2012, YouTuber Laina Morris uploaded a webcam video of her performing a parody of Justin Beiber’s single “Boyfriend” with personalized lyrics. The parody, with its clingy and stalker-like lyrics became viral and in less than 48 hours after its upload had received more than 1.35 million views. The next day on the 7th of June, Redditor yeahhtoast submitted a link to the video in a Reddit thread titled “Overly Attached Girlfriend” and within a few hours an advice animal character had been created with the same name.

  • 2010

    "That really rustled my jimmies"

    On June 16th, 2010, an image macro of a frowning gorilla with the overlaid text “That really rustled my jimmies” was posted on FunnyJunk. On December 15th, 2010, a thread was made on 4chan’s /r9k/ board about “times when someone really rustled your jimmies”. It was told in the style of a normal greentext story on 4chan, except using the outdated language from the 1950s. The expression and meme became known to be used to express feelings of discontent or discomfort in response to someone else’s post.
